The garage is the only room in your home with no rules. No one tells you where things go. No one designed it for daily life.

✦ Step One
🥄 Ingredient #1 — Edit with Intention

Before You Buy
a Single Bin

The biggest mistake people make when organizing a garage? They go shopping before they edit. You end up with beautiful bins full of things you don't actually need.

Before touching anything, ask yourself these 3 questions:

Lorena's 3 Questions

1
Do I actually use this — or am I keeping it "just in case"?
2
If I needed this tomorrow, would I even know where to look for it?
3
Does this belong in the garage, or did it just end up here?

Your Edit Categories

✓
Keep Used regularly, belongs here
✓
Donate Good condition, someone needs it more
✓
Trash Broken, expired, no life left
✓
Relocate Belongs somewhere else in the house
✦ Step Two
🥄 Ingredient #2 — Design for Real Life

Design Your Garage
for Real Life

Organization only works when it's built around how you actually live — not how you wish you lived. Every garage needs 4 clear zones.

Zone 01
Daily Access
Things you grab weekly. Eye level, easy reach, closest to the door.
Zone 02
Seasonal Storage
Holiday decor, camping gear. Upper shelves or back wall. Always labeled.
Zone 03
Sports & Hobbies
Grouped by activity. If your kids can't put it back themselves, simplify the system.
Zone 04
Tools & Hardware
Pegboards, bins, magnetic strips. Small parts in labeled containers. Visible = usable.

Label everything like someone else has to find it.

✦ Step Three

The 4-Hour
Garage Reset Plan

You don't need a full weekend. You need a plan. Here's how to do this in one focused afternoon.

Hour 1

Edit Everything Out

Pull everything out of the garage. Yes, everything. Sort into your 4 edit categories. Don't second-guess — keep moving.

Hour 2

Clean the Space

Sweep the floor. Wipe down shelves. A clean surface changes how you see — and design — the space.

Hour 3

Define Your Zones

Place items back by zone, not by category. Think: how do I actually use this space day to day?

Hour 4

Label, Finish, Celebrate

Label every bin, shelf, and zone. Take your after photo. You did it.

What You'll Need — Keep It Simple

Clear plastic bins (uniform size — don't mix)
A label maker or white labels + black marker
A pegboard or wall hooks for tools
That's it. Start there.
✦ Step Four
🥄 Ingredient #3 — Sustainable Systems

How to Keep It
This Way

The goal isn't a perfect garage. The goal is a garage you can maintain without thinking about it.

🔄
The One In, One Out Rule
Every time something new comes into the garage, something old leaves. No exceptions.
📅
The 15-Minute Seasonal Reset
Twice a year — spring and fall — walk through your zones. Takes 15 minutes when your system is already in place.
👨‍👩‍👧
Make It Family-Proof
If your kids or partner can't put things back without asking where they go, your labels aren't clear enough. Simplify until it's obvious.
