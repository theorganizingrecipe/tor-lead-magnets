That's exactly where The Organizing Recipe comes in. Same method. Every time. No judgment — just a process.

The TOR Method: How We Always Start

🥄

The golden rule: Organization is not about perfection. It's about creating a system that actually works for the people living in that home — their schedule, their habits, their real life.

🏠

Start with the most congested, most-used areas

We don't start where it looks easiest. We start where life actually happens — the spaces the family uses every single day. That's where the biggest relief comes from, and that's what keeps the momentum going.

TOR Principle
🧹

Floor first. Always.

Before anything else, we clear the floor. This is non-negotiable. You can't work in a space you can't move through. Creating physical space is what allows the process to begin — mentally and physically.

Step 01
🗂️

Remove trash + build piles by category

Trash out first — no hesitation. Then we pull everything that doesn't belong in that room and start grouping by category. This is where you begin to see what you actually have, often for the first time in years.

Step 02
✅
Keep
It has a home and it earns its place
🧡
Donate
Good condition, someone else needs it more
🗑️
Trash
Expired, broken, or no longer serving anyone
💡

Pro tip on paperwork: Documents that need time to review — don't let them block your momentum. Create a "pending pile," set it aside, and come back to it once the space is organized. Process the space first, then the papers.

Day 01
Master Bedroom + Closet
🛏️

Master Bedroom — Clearing the Foundation

Floor first. Then the bed — remove all clothing and objects that don't belong there. Once everything is off every surface, we categorize and make decisions. What stays gets a dedicated home. What doesn't have one? We create it.

Bedroom Reset
👗

Closet — Drawer by Drawer, Category by Category

Everything comes out. T-shirts, pants, dresses, accessories — each category is reviewed, edited, and given its own dedicated zone. The rule that never changes: what you use every day lives at eye level. Everything else finds its place — or it leaves.

Closet System
👁️

The eye-level rule: If you have to dig for it, you won't maintain it. Daily-use items live between shoulder and hip height — visible, accessible, effortless. Everything else is stored by frequency of use.

Day 02
Entryway · Dining Room · Kitchen · Pantry
🚪

Entryway + Dining Room — Redesigning for Real Life

The dining room had become the homeschool zone. Instead of fighting that reality, we honored it — and redesigned the space so both functions could coexist with intention. Every zone got a clear purpose. Clutter out, function in.

Space Redesign
🍳

Kitchen — Built for a Family That Cooks

This family cooks — really cooks. So the kitchen layout had to reflect that. The workflow, the counter space, the accessibility of tools — everything was reorganized around how they actually use this kitchen every day. Not how a magazine would style it. How they live in it.

Kitchen System
🥫

Pantry — Full Reset with a System for Everyone

Everything came out. Expired items: gone. Good products they no longer wanted: donated. What remained was organized into intentional zones — a snack area at kids' height, a breakfast station, a dinner prep area, a baking section. Every item accessible, every zone logical, every family member served.
